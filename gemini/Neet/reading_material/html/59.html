The joint where your thumb connects to your wrist is called the carpometacarpal joint. This specific joint, the one between the trapezium bone (in your wrist) and the first metacarpal (your thumb bone), is a special type called a saddle joint.

Think of a rider sitting in a saddle - that's how this joint works! The trapezium bone has a concave (hollowed-out) surface that the thumb bone, with its convex (rounded) surface, fits perfectly into. This unique shape lets your thumb move in many directions: you can touch your fingertips, bend it, move it away from your hand, and more!

This special joint is the reason we have such incredible dexterity and can grasp objects with our hands.
