Our bodies are supported by a strong, bony framework called the skeleton. It's divided into two main parts: the axial skeleton, which forms the central axis of the body, and the appendicular skeleton, which includes the bones of our limbs.

The axial skeleton includes the skull, vertebral column, and rib cage. The skull, which protects our brain, is made up of several bones, including the sphenoid bone.

The appendicular skeleton is responsible for movement and mobility. It includes the bones of our arms, hands, shoulders, legs, feet, and hips.

Remember: The sphenoid bone is a key part of the skull, which is part of the axial skeleton. Therefore, it is not part of the appendicular skeleton.
