The clavicle, also known as the collarbone, is a unique bone with an "S" shape. It has two distinct curves, one facing towards the sternum (breastbone) and the other facing towards the shoulder. This bone plays a vital role in supporting the shoulder, enabling arm movement, and protecting vital structures.

The key takeaway is that the clavicle is a dermal bone, meaning it develops from the skin, and its medial curve interacts with the sternum.
