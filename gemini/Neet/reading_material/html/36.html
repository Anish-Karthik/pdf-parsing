The human rib cage is a vital protective structure, made up of 12 pairs of ribs, the sternum, and vertebrae. While most ribs connect directly to the sternum, some are attached indirectly or not at all.

Here's the breakdown:

  • True Ribs (1-7): Attached directly to the sternum via cartilage.
  • False Ribs (8-10): Connected to the 7th rib through cartilage, not directly to the sternum.
  • Floating Ribs (11-12): Only attached to the vertebrae, not the sternum.

The term "vertebrochondral ribs" specifically refers to the false ribs (8-10).

The Incorrect Match:

The statement "Vertebrochondral ribs - 11th and 12th pair of ribs" is incorrect. The 11th and 12th ribs are floating ribs, not vertebrochondral.
