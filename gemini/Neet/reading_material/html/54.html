The Pectoral Girdle: Your Upper Limb's Foundation

The pectoral girdle acts like a bridge, connecting your upper limbs to your torso. It's made up of two strong bones on each side: the clavicle (collarbone) and the scapula (shoulder blade).

Clavicle: This S-shaped bone runs horizontally across your chest, linking the scapula to your breastbone (sternum).

Scapula: This triangular bone sits on your back, nestled against your ribcage. It has a prominent bump called the acromion, which forms a joint with the clavicle.

The Glenoid Cavity: Where the Magic Happens

The scapula also has a special feature: the glenoid cavity. This shallow, cup-shaped socket on the scapula's outer side is where the head of your humerus (upper arm bone) fits snugly. This connection forms the shoulder joint, which allows for incredible flexibility.

Remember: While the glenoid cavity is essential for the shoulder joint, it's not a separate component of the pectoral girdle. It's a part of the scapula, one of the two bones that make up the pectoral girdle.
