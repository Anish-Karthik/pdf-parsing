The human skeleton is a marvel of engineering, providing structure, support, and movement. Each arm is a complex system of 30 bones, working together to give us the dexterity and strength we need to interact with the world around us.

Let's break down the arm's bony structure:

  • Shoulder: The shoulder joint is where the arm meets the body. It's made up of the scapula (shoulder blade) and clavicle (collarbone).
  • Upper Arm: The upper arm contains the humerus, the long bone connecting the shoulder and elbow.
  • Forearm: The forearm has two bones, the radius and ulna. They work together to move the hand.
  • Wrist and Hand: The wrist contains 8 tiny bones called carpals. The hand itself has 5 metacarpals (palm bones) and 14 phalanges (finger bones).

These bones, along with the muscles and joints, create a powerful system that allows us to reach, grasp, and move with precision.

So, the answer is: Each arm of a human is made of 30 bones.
