Menopause and Your Bones: The Estrogen Connection

Menopause, a natural transition marking the end of a woman's menstrual cycle, brings a significant decrease in estrogen levels. This change has a big impact on bone health.

Why? Because estrogen is a powerful bone protector. It keeps our bones strong by:

  • Building bone: Estrogen fuels the activity of osteoblasts, the cells that create new bone.
  • Slowing bone breakdown: It also keeps osteoclasts, the cells that break down bone, in check.

When estrogen levels drop during menopause, this balance is disrupted. Osteoclasts become more active, leading to accelerated bone loss. At the same time, bone formation slows down.

This double whammy significantly increases the risk of osteoporosis, a condition where bones become weak and brittle, making fractures more likely.

The takeaway? The decrease in estrogen during menopause is a major factor contributing to osteoporosis.
