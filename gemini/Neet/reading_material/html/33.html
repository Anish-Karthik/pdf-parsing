Our bodies are amazing machines, and joints are key to their movement. Imagine tiny hinges that connect our bones, allowing us to bend and straighten our arms and legs. These are called hinge joints, and they're responsible for the movement of our knees and elbows.

Hinge joints allow for motion in just one direction, like opening and closing a door. They're crucial for everyday actions like walking, running, and even simply picking up a glass of water.

So, the next time you bend your knee or straighten your arm, remember that hinge joints are the unsung heroes of your movement!
