The axial skeleton forms the central axis of your body, providing support and protection for vital organs. It's made up of the skull, spine, and rib cage.

Within the thorax, the chest region, lies the sternum, also known as the breastbone. This flat bone is located on the ventral midline, which is the front center line of your body. The sternum connects to the ribs through costal cartilages, creating the front part of your rib cage.

Therefore, the flat bone of the axial skeleton that sits on the ventral midline of the thorax is the sternum.
