Our skeletal system is a complex and amazing framework that supports and protects our bodies. Let's explore some key parts and how they work together.

The sternum, or "breastbone," is a flat bone in the center of your chest. It acts as a central anchor point for the ribs, which form the protective cage around your heart and lungs.

These ribs are connected to the sternum by hyaline cartilage, a type of flexible connective tissue. This type of cartilage is called costal cartilage when it connects ribs to the sternum. It allows for a bit of flexibility in the rib cage, which is important for breathing. It also helps prevent the ribs from rubbing directly on the sternum, protecting the delicate tissues inside.
